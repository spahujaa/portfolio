Who I really am

Not just a
pixel pusher.

When I'm not pushing pixels, I'm chasing light with a camera — hunting for the perfect shot in cities I probably booked a flight to on a whim.

I'm a firm believer that the best ideas don't come from boardrooms; they come from the corner table of a café with good espresso and questionable Wi-Fi.

Travelling feeds my eye for composition, strangers' interiors fuel my design instincts, and an iced latte buys me two solid hours of hyperfocus.

Somewhere between a delayed flight, a latte art masterpiece, and golden hour in an unfamiliar street, I do my best thinking. My camera roll is part architecture, part weird selfies and part coffee cups — and I have zero plans to fix that.
